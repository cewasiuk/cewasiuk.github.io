About

I am a Physics PhD researcher at UC Santa Cruz studying gravitational-wave and black-hole phenomenology, with a focus on dark sectors, black hole evaporation, superradiance, and signal processing for resonant microwave detectors. My work combines theoretical modeling with phase-coherent analysis pipelines (matched filtering and robust PSD estimation) for high-resolution experimental data, including projects connected to ADMX/LBNL efforts to explore MHz–GHz gravitational-wave searches with resonant cavities.

Research areas

Black hole evaporation and constraints on new physics; dark degrees of freedom and precision gravity; asteroid-mass PBH mergers and resonant microwave cavity response; phase-coherent signal processing and weak-signal inference in narrowband detectors.
